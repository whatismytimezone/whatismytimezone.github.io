What Does "Time Zone" Actually Mean?

A time zone is a region of the globe that observes a uniform standard time for legal, commercial, and social purposes. Most time zones are defined by an offset from Coordinated Universal Time (UTC). For example, if you are in London during the winter, you are at UTC+0, while someone in New York is at UTC-5.

Quick Fact:

There are currently over 38 different local time zones in use worldwide. Some countries, like India or Nepal, use offsets that are 30 or 45 minutes apart from the standard hour intervals!

Common Time Zone Abbreviations

  • GMT: Greenwich Mean Time
  • EST: Eastern Standard Time (UTC-5)
  • CET: Central European Time (UTC+1)
  • JST: Japan Standard Time (UTC+9)
